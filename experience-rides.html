ملاهي وعائلة Rides & Family

Bring the kids. Bring your teta.
Bring العيلة.

Twilight joy on the fairgrounds. Kids on the carousel, teenagers at the midway, grandparents in the shade by the bazaar. This is a multi-generational festival, designed to keep everyone — from the four-year-old to the eighty-year-old — comfortable until closing.

Family first

A festival built for the العيلة.

The OC Fairgrounds is a fair — which means rides. We didn't tear them out; we built the festival around them. Kids ride while you eat. Teens cluster at the midway while you watch the Festival Stage. Everyone meets back at the bazaar by sunset.

This is a family festival because MENA is a family culture. Every service you need to bring a four-year-old and a grandmother through a ten-hour day is on the grounds — and most of it is free. Kids under ten enter free with a ticketed adult. No limit. Bring the whole family.

The Midway · الألعاب

Thirty rides. One Ferris wheel at golden hour.

Giant Wheel · golden-hour hero

The Giant Wheel — the قلب of the grounds.

One hundred and fifty feet up, with a view of the full festival and the Pacific beyond. It lights up at dusk and keeps turning until midnight. Go at golden hour — trust us. You'll see your whole weekend laid out beneath you.

Kids Zone · ركن الأطفال

A whole quarter of the grounds للأطفال.

The Kids Zone sits just north of the carousel — fenced, shaded, staffed, and loud in the best way. Drop in for an hour while the teens do their thing, or park here for the afternoon. Everything inside is free with a festival ticket.

Staffed by vetted children's educators and bilingual volunteers. Wristbands at every entry with your phone number on the back — lost kids walk straight to the Family Services booth at the center.

  • رسم
    Face Painting

    Henna-inspired swirls, festival-logo cheek stamps, butterflies, superheroes — artists set up at three stations.

  • حنة
    Henna for Kids

    Temporary, 10-minute designs on hands or arms — safe natural henna, done by professional artists with tiny chairs for tiny clients.

  • صنع
    Craft Stations

    Tile-pattern coloring sheets, Arabic-letter stamping, lantern making, bead bracelets — three stations, rotating daily.

  • حكاية
    Storytelling Tent

    Bilingual storytime three times a day — Arabic folk tales retold in English and Arabic. Pillows, low benches, cushions from the bazaar.

  • لعب
    Inflatable Play Village

    Bounce houses, soft obstacle course, and a shaded toddler area for the under-fives. Socks required.

  • هدوء
    Sensory-Friendly Hour

    Sunday 11am–noon: rides run slower, volume turned down, crowds thinned. For neurodivergent kids and their families.

For the parents · للأهل

Bring your teta. Bring your جدو.

We thought of the small things — the ones that decide whether your mother stays until the headliner or asks to leave at seven. Elder seating at every stage. A quiet musalla at the heart of the grounds. Full ADA pathways. Shade everywhere you'd want it.

  • مصلى
    Prayer Space (Musalla)

    Two quiet, carpeted rooms with wudu facilities near the bazaar center. Jummah at 1 PM Friday. Open all weekend.

  • راحة
    Elder-Friendly Seating

    Reserved shaded seating blocks at every stage — first come, first served, clearly marked, near cold-water stations.

  • وصول
    Full Wheelchair Access

    ADA pathways across the grounds, accessible parking at East Entry, free loaner wheelchairs at every info booth.

  • لغة
    Arabic + ASL Support

    Bilingual staff at every info booth. Sign language interpretation at headliner sets. Arabic-speaking medics on call.

  • أمومة
    Nursing + Family Rooms

    Private, air-conditioned rooms with changing tables, outlets, and a quiet corner — in the Family Services building.

  • عربة
    Stroller & Wagon Parking

    Free, staffed stroller and wagon parking at the East Entry. Rental wagons available on site for a small deposit.

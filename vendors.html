Common questions

Vendor FAQ.

Do I need insurance to vend?

Yes. All vendors must carry a Certificate of Insurance (COI) with minimum $1M general liability, listing OC MENA Festival and the OC Fair & Event Center as additional insured. We can point you to vendors who issue short-term event coverage if you don't already have a policy.

What permits do I need?

Food vendors need an Orange County Health Department Temporary Food Facility permit plus a valid California food handler certificate. Merchandise vendors need a California Seller's Permit (free from CDTFA). We handle the fairground-side permits; you handle the vendor-side ones, and we walk you through each step after acceptance.

What are the hours?

Gates open to the public at 4 PM Friday, 11 AM Saturday, and 11 AM Sunday. Vendor setup starts at 9 AM each day; breakdown is optional after 10 PM Friday and Saturday, required Sunday night after 11 PM. Full weekend stall fees cover all three days — no single-day spots.

When is load-in and setup?

Load-in runs Thursday June 18 from 9 AM to 8 PM. A dry-run walkthrough happens Friday morning before gates. All stalls must be fully built out by noon Friday. Overnight security is provided for all three nights on festival grounds.

Can I sell at one day only?

No. Vendor spots are three-day commitments. If you can only cover part of the weekend, we recommend partnering with another small vendor to split the stall — we're happy to help match you.

Is there a commission on sales?

No commission. You pay the flat stall fee and keep 100% of your sales. We'll ask you to log rough sales volume at end-of-day so we can improve placement and traffic flow for 2027 — this data is anonymous and never shared publicly.

Do I bring my own tent?

Stalls include a festival-branded 10×10 tent with weather cover, a 6-foot table, two chairs, and one 20-amp power drop. Food-track stalls include a handwashing station within 50 feet. Additional furnishings, signage, refrigeration, and decor are up to you.

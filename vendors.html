The process · الخطوات

Four steps from apply to serving.

01

Apply

Fill the form below. Tell us who you are, what you make, and why it belongs in this souq. Photos help.

02

Review

Our bazaar lead Razan Kesbeh reviews every application. Decisions sent within 3 weeks. If we need a taste test, we'll ask.

03

Contract & permits

We handle fair-side permits. You handle food handler certificates, business license, COI. We walk you through it.

04

Set up & sell

Load-in Thursday June 18. Dry runs Friday morning. Gates open Friday 4 PM. Let it rip.

What we're looking for · ممن نبحث عنهم

Six kinds of stalls.

Full kitchen · مطبخ كامل

Mezze, tagine, mandi, koshari, shawarma — restaurants or caterers serving complete meals from a 10x20 stall with full prep & cook setup.

Fee: $— · Full health permit

Sweets & bakery · حلويات

Knafeh, baklava, kunafa, ma'amoul, halawet el jibn, kaak — bakers and dessert shops with 10x10 display + serve stalls.

Fee: $— · Health permit

Drinks & coffee · مشروبات وقهوة

Arabic coffee, Turkish coffee, karak, mint lemonade, fresh-pressed juice, jallab, tamr hindi. Roasters also welcome.

Fee: $— · Service only permit

Artisan goods · صناعات يدوية

Jewelry, ceramics, textiles, leather, calligraphy, candles, ouds, cosmetics, prayer goods. Artists and makers only — no re-sellers.

Fee: $— · Seller's permit

Clothing & textiles · ملابس

Modern kaftans, keffiyehs, embroidered thobes, abayas, streetwear with MENA identity, handmade scarves and wraps.

Fee: $— · Seller's permit

Services & experiences · خدمات وتجارب

Henna artists, calligraphers taking custom names, caricature artists, tea ceremony, oud oil bar, Arabic name meaning booths.

Fee: $— · Business license

Stall fees are custom — we price based on stall size, location on grounds, and weekend vs single-day. Small-business and first-generation maker discounts apply. Ask us.

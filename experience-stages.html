المسارح Stages

Three stages. Three tempos.
One مهرجان.

Every stage is an architectural void carved into the fairgrounds — calligraphic arches, carved-wood proscenia, lantern-lit rigging. Each one is built for a different moment of the night.

3 Stages 13,700+ Combined Capacity Arabic Calligraphy Design
The architectural void

Every stage is a carved قوس.

Our three stages were designed with a single brief: build an Arabic calligraphic motif into the architecture itself, so the proscenium is doing half the design work before a single musician takes the stage. The main amphitheatre's wings are shaped around the word وطن ("homeland"). The Festival Stage carries a Kufic-script frieze across its crown. The Indoor Stage's backdrop is a diwani flourish that reads differently depending on where you stand.

The Pacific Amphitheatre is the headline moment — eight thousand seats under the stars. The Festival Stage is the all-day outdoor pulse. The Indoor Stage is where the weekend keeps going after the last headliner says goodnight.

Headliners PAC Amphitheatre at dusk · hero shot
Main Stage المدرج الرئيسي

Pacific Amphitheatre.

The 8,000+ seat amphitheatre inside the fairgrounds — one of Southern California's most storied outdoor music venues. Curved seating, line-array sound, sightlines engineered for every section. Saturday and Sunday nights, this is where the headliners land. The roof wings are carved with the word وطن — homeland — rendered so large it reads as silhouette from the top row.

وطن "Homeland." Carved into the stage's roof wings, visible from every seat in the house.

The Festival Stage.

The beating heart of the fairgrounds. Open-air, standing-room, all-day programming Saturday and Sunday, and the Friday headliner location. Festival ticket holders get full access — no upgrade required. The Festival Stage is where you'll end up whether you planned to or not. Its Kufic-script frieze runs the full length of the proscenium arch — calligraphy you only notice once you stop and look up.

موسيقى "Music." Spelled in Kufic script across the proscenium's stone frieze.

The Indoor Stage.

A 1,200-capacity indoor venue with full air conditioning — the festival's second act. Built for stand-up comedy, acoustic sets, poetry showcases, and the smaller-room moments that need a close-range crowd. The stage backdrop is a carved diwani flourish that shifts meaning depending on where you sit — from the front rows it reads as a single swooping line; from the back it resolves into the word كلمة ("word").

كلمة "Word." A diwani flourish across the backdrop — only legible once you step back a few rows.

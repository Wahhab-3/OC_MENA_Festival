سوق الطعام Food & Bazaar

Taste the whole region —
in one weekend.

A 600-foot سوق stretching the length of the fairgrounds. Food at one end, artisans at the other, music echoing through the middle. Ten MENA cuisines, a hundred vendors, every hand a story.

100+ Vendors 10 Cuisines Halal-Certified
The idea

The bazaar isn't a side-quest. The سوق is the spine of the festival.

Every dish is MENA-made. Every stall is MENA-owned. Walk east to west and you've crossed the region — Beirut to Baghdad, Cairo to Casablanca, Sana'a to the Horn. Music drifts through from the stages, orange blossom from the knafeh pans, cardamom from the coffee carts.

We curated this bazaar with Razan Kesbeh as our bazaar lead. The rule was simple: family recipes over franchises, hands over machines, line-worthy dishes over line-item menus. Halal-certified unless clearly labeled otherwise. Cash optional — most vendors are cashless, but the tea ladies and the henna booth appreciate it.

What you'll eat · الأكل

Eat your way across the region.

Eight kitchens, eight regional accents, one afternoon if you pace yourself. You won't.

Lebanon Lebanese mezze · grill · photo
مقبلات ومشاوي
Lebanese Shawarma & Mezze
Beirut · the Levant

Hummus, tabbouleh, fattoush, labneh with olive oil, sujuk off the grill, shish tawook, kofta, manakish folded in paper — the whole table, eaten with your hands.

Egypt Koshari counter · photo
من مصر
Egyptian Koshari
Cairo · Alexandria

The working-class street-food classic: rice, lentils, chickpeas, macaroni, crispy onions, spicy tomato sauce. Plus hawawshi, ful medames, ta'ameya, roz bel laban.

Morocco Tagine and mint tea · photo
من المغرب
Moroccan Tagine
Marrakech · Fes · Casablanca

Lamb tagine with prunes and almonds, chicken bastilla, harira, msemmen folded hot, and mint tea poured from three feet up. The tea-pour is part of the show.

Yemen Mandi pit · clay oven · photo
من اليمن
Yemeni Mandi
Sana'a · Aden

Lamb or chicken mandi cooked in underground clay ovens, pulled out just before they plate it. Served with bint al sahn, honey, and saltah on the side. Cooked in the ground the way it's supposed to be.

Palestine Musakhan rolls + knafeh · photo
من الأرض
Palestinian Musakhan
Nablus · Jerusalem · Jenin

Sumac-roasted chicken on taboon bread with caramelized onions, toasted pine nuts, olive oil. Plus maqluba, za'atar man'ousheh, and the knafeh kitchen — the stall with the line.

Iraq Iraqi kebab · masgouf grill · photo
من العراق
Iraqi Kebab & Masgouf
Baghdad · Mosul · Basra

Hand-chopped kebab over charcoal, dolma wrapped tight, kubba Mosul the size of a fist, amba on the side. And masgouf — the river-fish tradition that's older than most cuisines alive.

Syria Damascus sweets + pastries · photo
من الشام
Syrian Sweets & Plates
Damascus · Aleppo · Homs

Kibbeh nayyeh, yabraq, muhammara, shawarma Dimashqi — plus the sweets stall: barazek with pistachio, kellaj in syrup, mabroumeh coiled by hand. The slow food of the Levant, made quick for a festival.

Gulf Gulf dates + coffee service · photo
الخليج
Gulf Dates & Coffee
Saudi · Kuwait · the UAE

Arabian coffee poured from a dallah, cardamom-forward, served the traditional way — with medjool, khalas, and sukkari dates. Plus Saudi kabsa and machboos on the grill alongside.

The Bazaar · السوق

More than food. A سوق of hands.

The artisan half of the souq runs 300 feet along the east side of the grounds — seven vendor categories, every stall MENA-owned or operated with deep community roots. The henna booth, the calligraphy table, the oud-cologne stand: all of them do their work in front of you.

مكسرات
Textiles

Kaftans, keffiyehs, embroidered thobes, Berber rugs. Folded on low tables.

خط
Calligraphy

Custom names, Quranic verses, family words — hand-drawn on the spot.

ذهب
Jewelry

Silver and gold, evil-eye, hand-of-Fatima, zar filigree, Khamsa pendants.

بهارات
Spices

Za'atar, baharat, ras el hanout, sumac, dried limes, saffron threads in tiny tins.

بيت
Home Goods

Blue Fez pottery, hand-painted tagines, brass lanterns, tea sets, Damascene bags.

عود
Perfumes & Oud

Oud oils, rosewater, amber blocks, incense burners still smouldering at the stall.

كتب
Books

Arabic poetry, children's bilingual books, cookbooks translated both ways.

Razan Kesbeh · Bazaar Lead

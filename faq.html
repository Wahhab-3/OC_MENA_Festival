Category · 01 التذاكر

Tickets & entry

When do tickets go on sale, and how does pricing work? +

Presale opens in early 2026 for our newsletter list — join at the signup form to get first access. General on-sale follows a few weeks later. Pricing is tiered: single-day, three-day, and VIP each priced to stay accessible; a limited early-bird tranche is the cheapest the tickets will ever be.

Are tickets physical, or just on my phone? +

Tickets are digital — delivered as a QR code to your email and mobile wallet. Screenshots work if your service cuts out at the gate. At entry you'll swap the QR for a wristband, which is your pass for the day (and re-entry).

Can I transfer or resell a ticket I can't use? +

Yes — transfers run through our ticketing partner and are free. Please don't sell on third-party resale sites: scalped tickets are the easiest way to end up with a bad QR at the gate, and we can't honor them.

If you're stuck, reach out through the contact page before transferring — we can often help.

What's included with VIP? +

VIP includes:

  • Express entry at both gates
  • Raised viewing platform at Festival Stage
  • Reserved seating section at the Pacific Amphitheatre
  • Dedicated VIP restrooms and shaded lounge
  • One complimentary drink per day (alcoholic or non-alcoholic)
  • A festival tote with a small surprise from our food vendors
What's the refund policy? +

The festival is rain or shine, so tickets are generally non-refundable once purchased. If we cancel or reschedule a day due to conditions outside our control, you'll get a refund or rollover credit, your choice. Most concerns are best solved by a transfer — see the transfer question above.

Category · 02 في الموقع

On-site & getting in

What time do gates open, and what if I arrive late? +

Gates open at 5:00 PM on Friday and 2:00 PM on Saturday and Sunday. Last entry each night is 10:00 PM. Arriving after 6 PM on Saturday is fine — there's a rideshare drop right at the south gate and the line usually moves in under fifteen minutes.

Full hours (plus stage timing) live on the Info page.

Can I leave and come back the same day? +

Yes — same-day re-entry is allowed as long as your wristband is intact. Don't cut it off, don't lose it. Your wristband is your pass.

Where do I park, and how much is it? +

On-site parking is $20 per day, cashless, available day-of. Lots open 90 minutes before gates. Overflow goes to the adjacent Fairview lot. If you'd rather skip the lot entirely, there's a dedicated Uber/Lyft zone at the south gate — easily the fastest way out after Saturday's PAC headliner.

What can I bring in — and what gets turned away at the gate? +

The short version: small clear bags, empty water bottles, sunscreen, phone chargers, a light layer for later. Not allowed: weapons of any kind, glass containers, outside food and drink (with medical/dietary exceptions), professional cameras with detachable lenses, drones, hookah, and anything sharp or flammable.

The full list — allowed and prohibited — is on the Info page.

Is there a place to pray? +

Yes. A dedicated prayer room is open throughout festival hours with wudu facilities, prayer mats, and qibla direction provided. Jummah on Friday at 1:00 PM is led by a local imam and open to all.

Category · 03 المأكولات

Food & drink

What kind of food will be there? +

The Souq runs across the grounds and spans the region: Levantine mezze, Yemeni mandi, Moroccan tajine, Egyptian koshari, Palestinian musakhan, Iraqi masgouf, Persian kebab, Somali sambusa, Sudanese ful, plus coffee from half a dozen traditions and sweets for days. Most dishes are made by family-run vendors — the people you'd normally have to drive an hour to eat with.

Full vendor list goes live a few weeks before the festival on the Food & Bazaar page.

Are there vegan, vegetarian, and gluten-free options? +

Yes — MENA food is naturally plant-forward (hummus, baba ghanoush, fatteh, mujadara, moutabal, tabbouleh, falafel, and stuffed grape leaves are the opening act, not an afterthought). Every vendor labels menu items, and we ensure each dietary category has strong options at multiple booths.

Is everything halal? +

All meat served on the grounds is halal-certified or sourced from halal-only vendors. If you have a specific question about a dish's preparation, our vendor staff are happy to walk you through it.

Is there alcohol? +

There's a 21+ wine & beer garden on the west side of the grounds. The rest of the festival is alcohol-free by design — this is a family event first, and we want kids, elders, and people who don't drink to feel at home everywhere else.

Can I bring outside food or drink? +

Empty water bottles: yes — refill stations are everywhere. Outside food and drink otherwise: no. We do make exceptions for medical and dietary needs (infant formula, allergy-safe meals, etc.) — just tell a gate staffer when you arrive.

Category · 04 العائلة

Family & kids

Is this really family-friendly? Can I bring my kids, my mom, my uncle? +

Yes — that's the whole point. The festival is built to be enjoyed by three generations at the same time. There's a dedicated family zone (Twilight Joy), rides and carnival games for kids, seated spaces for elders, prayer rooms, stroller parking, and food every member of your family will recognize.

How old do my kids have to be to get in, and is there a kids' ticket? +

Children three and under are free. Under 10 are free with a ticketed adult. Kids 10+ need their own ticket. The grounds are all-ages until 10 PM; after 11 PM the Indoor Stage becomes 18+.

What is there for kids to actually do? +

Rides, carnival games, face painting, a henna station, storytelling hours, craft workshops, and a petting zoo rotate through the Twilight Joy family zone across all three days.

Details on the Rides & Family page.

Can I bring a stroller? +

Yes — strollers and wagons are welcome and free. There's dedicated stroller parking at the East entry. The grounds are fully paved; pathways stay wide.

Category · 05 الوصول للجميع

Accessibility & comfort

Is the festival wheelchair accessible? +

Yes. All paths are paved and wide. ADA restrooms are at every amenity node. There are reserved ADA seating zones at the Pacific Amphitheatre and Festival Stage, and continuous ADA cart service runs from 5 PM to midnight. Use the Arlington (ADA / Family) gate for the most accessible entry.

Do you offer companion tickets or ASL interpretation? +

Yes to both. A companion ticket is available at no additional charge — request it through the contact form after you buy your ticket. ASL interpretation is provided at Pacific Amphitheatre headliner sets with 72-hour notice via the contact form.

Is there a quiet / sensory-friendly space? +

Yes — a sensory-friendly quiet room is open through festival hours at the First Aid node, separate from the prayer room. Lower lighting, softer sound, clear signage. For anyone — kids, adults, anyone who just needs a minute.

Can I bring my service animal? +

Yes, with handler documentation. Please note: pets (including emotional support animals that aren't task-trained service animals) are not permitted — the grounds will be hot, loud, and crowded.

Category · 06 الفنانون

Artists & programming

Who's playing, and when is the full lineup announced? +

The first wave is on the Music & Entertainment page — a mix of headline Arab-pop legends, rising diaspora voices, and cross-over artists redrawing the sound. Second-wave announcements roll out in the months leading up, with the full day-by-day schedule published about six weeks out.

How do I pitch myself or my band to play? +

We're actively looking for emerging MENA and MENA-diaspora artists — especially ones who don't usually get main-stage treatment. Send EPK, music links, and a short note through the contact page, subject line "Artist submission." We read everything.

Will there be DJs or after-hours sets? +

Yes — the Indoor Stage runs later than the outdoor stages, with DJ sets and curated diaspora club nights Friday and Saturday. Saturday goes until 2 AM. It turns 18+ after 11 PM.

Category · 07 الشركاء

Vendors, sponsors & getting involved

How do I apply to vend food or retail? +

Vendor applications are open on the Vendors page. We prioritize MENA family-run operators, first-time festival vendors, and businesses that couldn't easily afford a premium-festival booth fee — our tiered pricing is designed to reflect that.

For specific questions, Razan Kesbeh runs vendor relations — reach her through the contact page.

How does sponsorship work? +

Sponsorship tiers and activation options are on the Sponsors page. We build custom packages for brands who want to show up authentically — not logo-slap a stage. Osama Aresheh leads sponsorships; reach him through the contact page, subject line "Sponsorship."

Can I volunteer or work the festival? +

Yes. We open volunteer and paid crew applications in early 2026. Volunteers get a daily wristband, crew meals, and a festival tote; paid roles include production, hospitality, ADA support, and vendor runners. Sign up on the Partners page to be notified when applications open.

How can I support without being a vendor or sponsor? +

Buy a ticket. Bring your family. Tell your neighbor. Post a photo and tag us. Sign up for the newsletter so the second-wave artist announcements reach you first. The festival is being built by community — showing up is the contribution.

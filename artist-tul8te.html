Tul8te

Egypt · مصر

Egyptian. Masked. Anonymous by choice. His debut album "Tesh Shabab" gave Gen Z Arab youth a mirror they had never seen before.

Tul8te arrived with a mask and no explanation. Egyptian, anonymous, and writing songs that Egyptian Gen Z recognized immediately as their own inner monologue. His 2024 debut album "Tesh Shabab" — roughly translating to "the energy of youth" — landed in February 2024 as one of the most emotionally direct Arabic albums in recent memory. He writes about anxiety, loneliness, mental health, and the exhaustion of performing happiness for people who don't see you. Young Egyptians heard themselves in it instantly.

"Habeeby Leh" — Why My Love — became the signature track. Over one million monthly listeners followed. The mask stays on. The music holds.

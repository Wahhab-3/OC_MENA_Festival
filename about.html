A festival built by the people who needed it most.

A conversation that started in a car on the way to Las Vegas, New Jersey, and Toronto — and ended with 150 acres in Orange County.

Why we're building this

We don't need permission to put our music on a main stage.
We just need الوطن — the stage.

— The conversation that started OC MENA

The Origin البداية

A drive that got too long.

For years, seeing the artists we grew up on meant getting on a plane. Ehab Tawfik was in New Jersey. Mohammed Assaf was in Toronto. Dystinct was in Paris. If a MENA headliner was touring the United States at all, they were rarely routed through Southern California — despite one of the largest Arab and MENA diaspora populations in the country living here.

The idea for OC MENA Festival came out of that frustration. A group of friends, families, and organizers who kept driving, kept flying, kept asking why the show wasn't coming to us. A community big enough to fill amphitheatres, still waiting to be booked like it mattered.

"If no one's putting this show on in Orange County — we will."

What started as a conversation became a pitch. The pitch became a venue — the OC Fair & Event Center and the Pacific Amphitheatre, two of California's longest-running public event grounds. The venue became a lineup. The lineup became a festival. Three years later, it's 150 acres, three stages, and one of the largest MENA cultural gatherings in the United States.

The Vision الرؤية

Main-stage treatment, as a default.

MENA artists have spent decades being booked at a niche scale in the American market — filed under "world" or "international" and slotted into 300-cap rooms while their peers in hip-hop, Latin, and K-pop filled arenas. We refused that framing on day one.

The Pacific Amphitheatre isn't a compromise. It's the statement. You book a venue like this when you believe the audience deserves it — and we do. Issam Alnajjar crossed a billion streams. Dystinct is charting across Europe. Mohammed Assaf sells out amphitheatres from Amman to New Jersey. The audience has been here. The infrastructure is finally catching up.

Our vision for OC MENA is simple: program this festival the way Coachella programs Coachella. Treat the headliners like headliners. Build the stages like a real concert, not a cultural showcase. Put MENA pop, rock, rap, and dance music on a bill that says out loud — this is the main event.

"We are not the first MENA festival in North America. We won't be the last. But we are among the first to book it like it matters."

Orange County was always the right room. One of the largest Arab diaspora populations in the country. A family culture that still gathers around music. A venue that seats 8,000 in the amphitheatre and 20,000+ across the festival grounds. It just needed a host.

What we promise وعدنا

Three rules we won't break.

01

By the community, with scale

Every vendor, every artist, every producer on this festival has roots in the region or the diaspora. This isn't a cultural tour — it's a family gathering with better sound design.

02

Main-stage treatment

MENA artists get niche budgets in the US market. We refused that from day one. The Pacific Amphitheatre isn't a compromise — it's the statement. Book it like it matters, because it does.

03

A festival your mom will love

Teta-friendly food. Prayer space. Family lounge. Rides for the kids. Late-night DJs for the young crowd. The festival works for everyone in the car ride — not just the one who drove.

The Team الفريق

A room full of people who showed up.

OC MENA is produced by a coalition of organizers, sponsorship leads, programming directors, and vendor coordinators — most of them MENA-American, many of them Orange County residents, all of them volunteers before any of this was paid work. The festival is built in partnership with the OC Fair & Event Center, one of California's longest-running public event venues, and supported by community organizations across Southern California.

The public-facing leads you'll interact with: Osama Aresheh runs sponsorships, corporate partnerships, and keeps the festival's larger relationships on the rails — he's the reason the amphitheatre got booked. Razan Kesbeh runs vendor relations — from Lebanese food stalls to Palestinian jewelers, over a hundred vendors on the grounds, all of them her call. Full program, production, and community teams sit behind them, and we'll introduce the rest publicly as the festival approaches.

Every person with a staff shirt on site is here because they believed in this before anyone else did. If that sounds like you — volunteer, partner, vendor — the contact page is where to start. We read everything.

العائلة

This is a festival for the whole car ride.

The Arab kid who grew up in Anaheim translating lyrics in the back seat. The Moroccan grandmother who hasn't heard Dystinct live and should. The parents who want a night out with their kids — not away from them. The non-MENA neighbor curious about the music echoing out of the OC Fairgrounds. Come in. Welcome. This is the room.

Three days. Three stages. One weekend where Orange County sounds like home.
